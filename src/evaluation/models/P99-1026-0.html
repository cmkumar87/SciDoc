[1] This paper proposes a method for incrementally understanding user utterances whose semantic boundaries are not known and responding in real time even before boundaries are determined. [2] The method utilizes beam search to deal with the ambiguity of boundaries as well as syntactic and semantic ambiguities. [3] This paper proposed ISSS (incremental significant-utterance-sequence search), an integrated incremental parsing and discourse processing method that enables both the understanding of unsegmented user utterances and real-time responses. [4] A significant utterance (SU) in the user's speech is a phrase that plays a crucial role in performing the task in the dialogue. [5] User utterances are understood by finding a sequence of SUs and updating the belief state based on the sequence.
